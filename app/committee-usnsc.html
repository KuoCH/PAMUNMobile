USNSC

The United States National Security Council (USNSC) is an agency within the Executive Office of the President, established by the National Security Act in 1947 to advise the president on domestic, foreign, and military policies related to national security. The chairman of the USNSC is the president of the United States. Other members include the vice president and the secretaries of state and defense. Advisers to the USNSC are the chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, the director of the Central Intelligence Agency, and other officials whom the president may appoint with Senate approval. The staff of the USNSC is headed by a special assistant for national security affairs known as the national security adviser, who generally acts as a close adviser of the president. The USNSC provides the White House with a useful foreign policy-making instrument which is independent of the State Department.
