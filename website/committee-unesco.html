UNESCO

United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) is a specialized agency under the United Nations. It was founded in 1945 after the Second World War. The goal of UNESCO is to establish “intellectual and moral solidarity of mankind” and, in doing so, prevent the outbreak of another world war. According to its official publication, UNESCO’s mission is to contribute to the building of a culture of peace, the eradication of poverty, sustainable development and intercultural dialogue through education, the sciences, culture, communication and information.


Topic I: Building and strengthening resilience on coastal and small islands

Small Island Developing States (SIDS) are one of the priority groups of UNESCO. Surrounded by ocean, SIDS are particularly vulnerable to hydro-meteorological hazards and earthquakes. For example, Indian Ocean earthquakes and tsunamis in 2004 have caused serious damage to the coastal countries, and destroyed their local economic activities and cultural heritage. Global climate change also increases the possibility of extreme climate, which may lead to unpredictable natural disasters. Thus, a system to prevent and prepare for such disaster is needed. Fortunately, the improvement of technology has offered SIDS new opportunities to enhance their resilience. Through the use of technology, international financial systems and policy-making frameworks, UNESCO aims to help these small island states to build and strengthen their resilience by ensuring that their societies will not be threatened by natural disasters.

Topic II: Promoting intercultural dialogue for sustainable development

With the deadline of the Millennium Development Goals fast approaching, the global community is searching for new methods for sustainable development, in which culture is considered as a key element. For UNESCO, placing culture at the heart of development policy constitutes an essential investment in the world’s future. Taking into account that cultural diversity is the cornerstone of globalization, the preservation of indigenous and local cultures becomes important. While old heritages can be preserved through new technologies and methods, the process of doing so may lead to the creation of new cultures. It is UNESCO’s responsibility to provide guidelines for member states to build a framework and encourage intercultural dialogue for culture diversity.
