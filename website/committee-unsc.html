UNSC

The United Nations Security Council (UNSC) is one of the six principal organs of United Nations. The Security Council is charged with the maintenance of international peace and security. Composed of five permanent members and ten rotating members, the UNSC can establish peacekeeping operations, sanctions, and authorize military actions.


Topic I: Situation in Northern Mali

Northern Mali has long suffered from conflicts between different ethnic groups. The three main belligerents, namely the National Movement for the Liberation of Azawad (MLNA), the Islamist-nationalist and the government of Mali are under conflict due to different beliefs. A Peace agreement was reached with the French-ECOWAS victory; yet, it failed to sustain in the long term. Three months after armistice was established, the rebels withdrew from the agreement, claiming that the government did not honor its commitment to truce. The situation in Mali became more complicated when terrorist group and other countries were involved. The United Nations established the United Nations Multidimensional Integrated Stabilization Mission in Mali (MINUSMA) to stablize the area; still, the situation remains dirupted. Futher measures need to be implemented by the international community in order to maintain stability and safety in the area.

Topic II: Response mechanism for possible genocide

Genocide has been the saddest tragedy in human history. Innocent people are slaughtered due to their difference in race and beliefs. To prevent genocide from happening, the United Nations has set up frameworks, sent rapporteurs, and taken all kinds of actions. However, in the past, all the existing bodies seemed to fail in reacting to genocides in a timely manner. In the 1990s, the Rwanda Genocide and Bosnian Genocide had taken millions of lives away and torn countless families apart. In recent years, the war in Darfur has also ignited concerns regarding the situation. For all these years, the United Nations seems to be failing to respond to most cases instantly. How can the current framework of response be improved? Immediate discussion on the issue is needed to halt so the adversity would not occur again.
