UNODC

The United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) is a combination of the United Nations International Drug Control Programme (UNDCP) and the Center for International Crime Prevention. As one of the biggest programs under the General Assembly, UNODC dedicates itself to dealing with topics including but not limited to organized crime and trafficking, corruption, criminal justice, drug abuse prevention, and health. Recently, UNODC has been focusing on counter terrorism and the justice of criminal, which are the topics we will be discussing this time.


Topic I: Elimination of violence against women and children

Women and children are two vulnerable groups in the international society that have not yet been well protected even until now. Violence against Women (VAW) includes a variety of categories such as rape, honor killing, acid throwing, forced marriage, sexual harassment, forced prostitution, and others. Violence against children, or child abuse, can fit into four main forms including physical abuse, sexual abuse, psychological abuse, and neglect. The United Nations has set up regulations and protocols striving to solve these problems. However, there are still millions of women and children worldwide suffering from different kinds of abuse. Delegates are recommended to review past actions and strive for new solutions.

Topic II: Protection of the rights of those facing the death penalty

In ancient times, the death penalty was once regarded as the ultimate punishment of criminals. According to Amnesty International, however, more than 140 countries have now abolished the death penalty. The United Nations has engaged in actions to protect the rights of criminals that are sentenced to death. These actions include setting limitations to determine who can be sentenced to death, ensuring the rights of criminals in asking for pardon or commutation, and methods of carrying out the death penalty. Delegates are recommended to discuss what kind of rights should be included in a Resolution and how these rights can be implemented and ensured through the structure of the United Nations.
