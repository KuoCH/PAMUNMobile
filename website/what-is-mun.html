What is Model UN

What is Model United Nations?

Model United Nations is debate and document drafting student activity that models the actual political process utilized in the United Nations. Students become ambassador to the UN and take on a countries roles and policies. They have the opportunity debate against work alongside equally passionate students in order to resolve complex global issues. The topics discussed in a MUN conference mirror those actually debated in the UN and cover areas ranging from international peace and security, to human rights, to socio-economic development, and even environmental protection. With such a broad encompassment of fields, the UN requires more than experts on politics, government, or international affairs. As long as you have a willingness to try and improve the world, you are able to contribute, regardless of what you may have studied in school or where your expertise lies. This expertise and knowledge expands every MUN conference you attend. You will have met new people and gained a better appreciation of the challenging albeit important duty faced by United Nations.

Process of Debate

The ultimate goal for delegates in a committee is to reach a consensus and pass a resolution. A UN resolution is a formal text that embodies the international community's view and recommendation towards a particular issue. Keep in mind that the UN General Assembly resolution is not legally binding on member states, because the UN realizes the importance of each member state’s sovereignty. Although this non-mandatory nature is seen by many to be a major limitation, a resolution still carries the considerable weight of 193 member states voices behind it. For serious international issues, the UN Security Council also has the power to step in with military action or sanctions to enforce a resolution.

The process of debate, codified as a formal "Rules of Procedure (ROP)," of any MUN conference mimics the actual process of debate utilized in the UN General assembly, and it is often modified to facilitate delegates' drafting of their own UN resolutions. PAMUN's ROP is tailored to be comprehensive for experienced delegates yet easily approachable for new ones.

Generally speaking, the PAMUN's process of debate can be broken down into formal session and voting bloc. In formal session, delegates may share their ideas through moderated speeches, or in an unmoderated caucus. The moderated speech provides all delegates individual podium time to broadcast to the entire floor or respond to questions. In an unmoderated caucus, ROP is suspended and delegates may freely walk around to discuss with each other, as well as use electronic devices.

The earlier sessions are meant for sharing ideas and partitioning into different ideological blocs. By the beginning of the final session, each bloc should have a drafted resolution circulating on the floor for final discussion, amendments, and lobbying. In the last half hour, formal session is closed in order to enter voting bloc. At PAMUN, only one resolution may be passed per committee, and this requires approval by ⅔ majority. Hopefully, each committee will pass a resolution. This sort of cooperation may require compromise and concession, but that is ultimately what the spirit of the UN is about.

For experienced delegates, the full text for the Pan Asia MUN 2014 Rules of Procedure will be made available soon.
